Systematic Literature Study
- Deliverables:
    - Compare at least 4 different research papers (this is the absolute minimum if no more papers are available)
    - Provide a detailed documentation of the literature review as supplementary material to the report. This can be done using a
table, that clearly indicates which papers were assessed in which iteration and which of them were (not) included

- Methodology in short:
    - Snowballing using the given references as seeds
    - Keyword search (inclusion / exclusion criteria) based on selected venues and years
    ALWAYS document your process!

    Kitchenham, S. Charters, Guidelines for Performing Systematic Literature Reviews in Software Engineering (version 2.3), Technical Report, Keele University and University of
Durham, 2007.

    Claes Wohlin. 2014. Guidelines for snowballing in systematic literature studies and a replication in software engineering. In Proceedings of the 18th International Conference on
Evaluation and Assessment in Software Engineering (EASE '14). Association for Computing Machinery, New York, NY, USA, Article 38, 1–10.
